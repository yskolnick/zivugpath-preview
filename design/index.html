Warm and editorial, not a loud dating app.

A dignified, photo-forward profile that feels made — like a magazine feature, not a swipe card. Warmth comes from paper, portraiture, and type; color stays disciplined. Every choice below is drawn from the verified design research.

04

Where dating-app habits get adapted

The look borrows from the best modern apps; the mechanics answer to a shidduch audience. Each pattern is turned, not copied.

Swipe on photos
Consent reveal →

No judging a face in a stack. Details open when both sides choose to look closer.

Open browsing
Private by default →

Nothing is public and nothing is indexed. Privacy is the headline, not a buried setting.

Selfie verification
References & vouching →

Trust the way the community already does — a name that says "I know her," not a face scan.

The emailed PDF
One living link →

A profile you update once and share anywhere — always the current version, never a stale attachment. A PDF still exists for those who want one — dated, and pointing back to the link.
